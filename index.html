Preparation Hub

General resilience priorities that apply across most scenarios, tiered by urgency. Category-specific guidance is available in each risk card above.

Immediate
  • Know evacuation routes for earthquake, tsunami, wildfire, or flood risk in your area
  • Keep a basic emergency kit (water, food, first aid, flashlight, radio, medications)
  • Register for local official alert systems
This Week
  • Review household emergency fund and food/water buffer (1-2 weeks minimum)
  • Check insurance coverage for flood, fire, and property damage
  • Set your location in the Location & Alerts panel above for tailored notes
Ongoing
  • Follow official sources (NOAA, USGS, WHO, local emergency management) directly, not just this dashboard
  • Diversify food/energy supply where feasible if you rely on vulnerable supply chains
  • Revisit this page regularly — risk is cumulative, not just today's snapshot
Transparency & Methodology

Multi-source rule: every category is assessed against at least two independent authoritative sources (government/scientific agencies preferred). Where sources disagree on severity, timeline, or interpretation, the discrepancy is shown explicitly rather than averaged away.

Conservative reading: when sources diverge, the more cautious reading is used for the displayed risk level, to avoid understating risk for the sake of avoiding alarm.

Cumulative context: a quiet week does not reset a risk level. Multi-month trends (e.g. El Niño strengthening, geomagnetic field weakening) are tracked and reflected even when the latest data point looks calm.

Confidence: data freshness and source agreement vary by category — see each category's "Sources Compared" and "Discrepancies" fields for specifics.

Limitations: this is a public awareness tool, not an official warning system. Always follow official local emergency guidance over this dashboard.
