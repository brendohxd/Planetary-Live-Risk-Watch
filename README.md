# Earth Risk Watch

Transparent, data-driven global risk monitoring for awareness and preparation.

## What This Is

Earth Risk Watch is a public-facing dashboard that aggregates hard data across multiple Earth-impacting risk categories. It is designed to be honest, detailed, and useful for people who want to stay informed and prepared without sensationalism.

The project emphasizes:
- Multi-source verification
- Explicit discrepancy tracking
- Conservative readings on severity and timelines
- Event-level history (not just summaries)
- Trend and cumulative context
- Cross-referencing between risks (especially how they affect Infrastructure & Financial Strain)
- Practical preparation guidance

## Current Categories (11)

1. Seismic Activity
2. Volcanic Activity
3. Solar & Space Weather
4. Ocean Temperatures & Marine Heatwaves (incl. El Niño)
5. Sea Level Rise & Coastal Extremes
6. Weather Extremes, Drought & Heat Stress
7. Wildfire Activity & Air Quality
8. Military Conflict & Escalation Risks
9. Near-Earth Objects (NEOs)
10. Geomagnetic Field Stability & Core Dynamics
11. Infrastructure & Financial Strain
12. Nuclear Risk

## Key Principles

- **Data over opinion**: All assessments are grounded in primary sources (NOAA, USGS, ESA, CPC, SIPRI, Bulletin of the Atomic Scientists, etc.).
- **Discrepancies are shown**: Where sources differ, we flag it and default to the more conservative reading.
- **Context matters**: A quiet week does not erase multi-month or long-term building risks.
- **Cross-references**: Risks are interconnected. We explicitly note how one category amplifies or is amplified by others.
- **Preparation focused**: Every category includes practical preparation priorities.

## Current State

- The site is hosted on GitHub Pages.
- Data is updated manually for now (future versions will be driven by the Planetary Risk Matrix skill).
- The interface includes a visual Doomsday Clock, risk bar chart, and detailed category modals with events and cross-references.

## Technology

- Static site (HTML + Tailwind + Chart.js)
- Data stored in `risk-data.json`
- Designed to be lightweight and deployable on GitHub Pages

## Future Direction

- Stronger automated research via the Planetary Risk Matrix skill
- Richer event history and cascading impact analysis
- Location-based alerts and notification preferences
- Improved accessibility (dark/light mode, high-contrast, audio support)
